# Workload Analysis: Morningside Elementary Special Education Teacher
## Estimated Using National Research Data and Published State Frameworks

## Methodology

This analysis estimates the total weekly and annual workload for a special education teacher serving 21 K-4 students with IEPs at Morningside Elementary, using publicly available research data, state workload calculation frameworks, and national averages. Where ranges exist, **conservative (low-end) estimates** are used to avoid overstating the case — the actual workload is likely higher.

## Part B: Direct Instruction Time (IEP Service Minutes)

### Estimating Per-Student Service Minutes

National data on typical elementary IEP service minutes per week:

| Source | Reported Range |
|--------|---------------|
| Practitioner reports (ProTeacher Community, Wrightslaw) | 150-345 min/week per student |
| New York State minimum (resource room) | 180 min/week (3 hrs) |
| Typical reading + math services reported | 225 min reading + 120 min math = 345 min/week |

**Conservative estimate used: 150 minutes/week per student** (2.5 hours)
This assumes a mix of students — some receiving only 1 service area, others receiving multiple. This is deliberately on the low end.

| Metric | Calculation | Result |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Students | | 21 |
| Average service minutes/week/student | Conservative estimate | 150 min |
| **Total direct instruction minutes/week** | 21 x 150 | **3,150 min** |
| **Total direct instruction hours/week** | 3,150 / 60 | **52.5 hrs** |

**Note:** This single category alone (52.5 hours/week) already exceeds the total available hours for a full-time teacher (31 hours). This is because special education teachers serve students in groups, not 1:1. A typical pull-out group of 3-5 students means the teacher delivers 3-5 students' service minutes simultaneously.

### Adjusted for Group Instruction

| Metric | Calculation | Result |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Average group size | 3-4 students (conservative) | 3.5 |
| Adjusted instruction hours/week | 52.5 / 3.5 | **15.0 hrs** |

**15.0 hours/week of the teacher's time dedicated to direct instruction.**

## Part D: Paperwork and Documentation

Research by Vannest & Hagan-Burke (2010) found special education teachers spend an average of **5 hours per week on paperwork** ([link](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/0741932508327459)). This includes:

- Progress monitoring reports (quarterly/trimester for all 21 students)
- Data collection and analysis
- Service logs
- Behavior documentation
- Communication logs
- Medicaid billing documentation (where applicable)

The Illinois workload framework estimates approximately **2 hours per student annually** for IEP-related paperwork alone, which for 21 students = 42 hours/year or 1.17 hrs/week — but this is *on top of* daily data collection and progress monitoring.

**Estimate used: 5.0 hours/week** (based on Vannest & Hagan-Burke research average)

## Part H: What This Means

### At full-time:
The workload is tight but feasible. The teacher works slightly beyond contract hours (consistent with the NEA finding that special education teachers average 6 extra hours/week), but can deliver services, meet compliance deadlines, and maintain relationships with students, families, and staff. This is the **baseline expectation**.

### At half-time:
**Over half the required work cannot be done.** The teacher must choose which obligations to cut:

- Skip direct instruction for some students? → **IEP violation, FAPE denial**
- Skip IEP meetings or delay them? → **IDEA timeline violation**
- Skip progress monitoring? → **Compliance violation, inaccurate parent reporting**
- Skip collaboration with general education teachers? → **Student outcomes suffer, inclusion fails**
- Skip SB 241 literacy team participation? → **State law violation**
- Skip parent communication? → **Relationship breakdown, due process risk**

**There is no combination of cuts that doesn't create legal liability or harm students.**

## Part I: Assumptions and Limitations

These estimates use **conservative, low-end figures** from national research:

| Assumption | Conservative Estimate Used | Actual Range in Research |
|-----------|---------------------------|-------------------------|
| Service minutes/week/student | 150 min | 150-345 min |
| Group size for instruction | 3.5 students | 1-5 students |
| IEP meeting length | 75 min | 45-120 min |
| IEP prep time | 2 hrs/student | 2-4 hrs/student |
| Evaluation time | 8 hrs/student | 8-20 hrs/student |
| Paperwork | 5 hrs/week | 5-8 hrs/week |
| % of special education students below reading benchmark | 71% | 72-74% (NAEP) |

**If actual Morningside data were substituted, the gap would almost certainly be larger.** These estimates can be refined with school-specific IEP service minutes, which would strengthen the analysis further.
